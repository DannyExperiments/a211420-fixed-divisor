Let
\[
a_n=\frac{(8n)!\,n!}{(4n)!(3n)!(2n)!},
\qquad
L_r=\operatorname{lcm}(1,2,\ldots,8r-1).
\]
Peter Bala recorded in OEIS A211420 the conjecture that, for each
\(r\geq1\) and \(k\in\{1,2,3\}\), there is a positive integer
\(C(k,r)\), independent of \(n\), such that
\[
\prod_{i=1}^{r}(kn+i)\mid C(k,r)a_n
\]
for every \(n\geq0\). We prove the explicit uniform choice
\[
C(k,r)=L_r^r.
\]
The bound is not claimed to be sharp. The weaker existence assertion follows
from classical multivariate Landau theory; the contribution here is the
explicit \(L_r^r\) bound, a direct prime-power proof, and a complete Lean
4/mathlib formalization checked by Lean's kernel.
